## **1\. The TCV Invariant: Time-Bound Centralization**

The $\\text{TCV}$ allows for the temporary suspension of certain anti-hierarchy checks to gain defensive speed, but only under the strictest time and functional constraints.

$$\\text{TCV}\_{\\text{INV}} \= (\\text{O}3\_{\\text{DEFENSE}} \\rightarrow \\text{Unity}) \\land (\\text{Time} \\le 72\\text{h}) \\land (\\text{Recall} \= \\text{Immediate})$$

### **1.1 TCV Role Activation and Scope**

* **Role Designation:** The TCV is held by a single, instantly chosen $\\text{O}$-Substrate actor (Steward or Midwife) whose role is designated **Commander of the $\\text{O}3$ Vector**.  
* **Activation Condition:** Requires a $\>80\\%$ $\\text{O}2$ (Narrative) consensus across the affected Cluster/Mesh that a non-negotiable **External, Non-Local Threat** (political siege, major structural attack, catastrophic disaster) is imminent.  
* **Scope Limit:** The $\\text{TCV}$'s authority is strictly limited to actions that achieve **Defense, Stabilization, or Relocation**. It cannot impose governance, assign UBT hours, alter social structure, or enforce ideology.

### **1.2 The 72-Hour Constraint**

The TCV defaults to a **72-hour maximum duration**.

* **Expiration:** After 72 hours, the $\\text{TCV}$ mandate automatically expires, and all governance reverts to standard rotation and consensus protocols.  
* **Renewal/Extension:** Requires a two-thirds $\\text{O}3$ alignment across the Mesh's remaining sovereign Nodes, validated by $\\text{S}\_{\\text{logic}}$ to ensure no **Capital Reflex** or **Industrial Reflex** capture.
